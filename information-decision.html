Five Steps of Decision-Making Process

Systematic approach to making effective business decisions

1

Identify the Problem

Find the problem and its root causes

Clearly define what needs to be solved and understand underlying issues

2

Generate Alternatives

Generate alternative solutions

Brainstorm multiple possible solutions and approaches to the problem

3

Evaluate Options

Analyze and compare alternatives

Assess pros and cons, costs, benefits, and feasibility of each option

4

Make Decision

Reach a decision, conduct ethics double-check

Choose the best alternative and ensure it aligns with ethical standards

5

Implement & Evaluate

Implement the decision and evaluate results

Put the decision into action and monitor outcomes for effectiveness

Note: Repeat the process if results are not satisfactory

Group Decision-Making

Collaborative approach to decision-making with both benefits and challenges

‚úÖ

Advantages

  • Diversity of ideas and perspectives
  • Fulfill social needs of team members
  • Improve internal communication
  • Increase commitment to decisions
  • Better problem-solving through collaboration
  • Shared responsibility and ownership
‚ùå

Disadvantages

  • Potential for conflicts and disagreements
  • Conformity and groupthink risks
  • Time-consuming process
  • Individual domination by strong personalities
  • Vagueness of responsibility
  • Difficulty reaching consensus

Individual vs Group Decision-Making

Aspect Individual Decision-Making Group Decision-Making
Speed Fast and efficient Slower due to discussion
Creativity Limited to one perspective Multiple perspectives and ideas
Accountability Clear individual responsibility Shared or unclear responsibility
Implementation May face resistance Higher commitment and buy-in
