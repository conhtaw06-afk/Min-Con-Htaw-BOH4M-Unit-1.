What is an Organization?

An organization is a structured group of people working together to achieve common goals and objectives. It involves coordinated activities, defined roles, and systematic processes to transform inputs into valuable outputs for stakeholders.

Productivity

The measure of how efficiently resources are converted into outputs. Calculated as Output √∑ Input, it shows the relationship between what is produced and the resources used.

Effectiveness

The degree to which organizational goals and objectives are achieved. It measures "doing the right things" and focuses on goal accomplishment rather than resource usage.

Goal Achievement

Efficiency

The ability to minimize resource waste while maintaining output quality. It focuses on "doing things right" by optimizing processes and reducing unnecessary costs.
